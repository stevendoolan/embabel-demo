# Man vs Ant

## The Coffee Crisis

Steven stared at the coffee machine in horror. At 7am on this Sunday morning, 12/04/2026,
the software engineer discovered his worst nightmare: ants marching across the water tank
like tiny commandos on parade. The Datadog t-shirt he'd worn for three days straight hung
loose over his pyjama shorts as he leaned closer, adjusting his glasses.

"Not the coffee machine," Steven muttered, watching the invaders contaminate his sacred
morning ritual. His wife had taken the kids to visit her parents in Sydney for the school
holidays, leaving him alone to face this crisis. No one to witness his war.

## The Arsenal

Steven remembered Easter Monday, six days earlier. He'd spotted the first scouts in the
kitchen, dismissed them as a minor bug in the system. A quick trip to Bunnings Stafford
had armed him with Mortein Power Gard—the expert's DIY solution, according to the teenager
in the pest control aisle.

That first assault had seemed successful. Steven had sprayed every wall junction, every
visible ant, even lifted storage boxes searching for their base. By sunset on 06/04/2026,
he'd declared victory. "Job done," he'd texted his wife, along with a thumbs-up emoji.

## The Escalation

But now, on this humid Brisbane morning with the temperature already hitting 27°C, Steven
faced the truth. The ants hadn't retreated—they'd regrouped. Outside on the concrete patio,
under the tin roof that amplified every raindrop, Steven discovered their true numbers.

The butcher birds—Tina, Gina, and Zina—watched from the mango tree as Steven, still in his
slides with zero protective equipment, began lifting items. A plastic tub revealed hundreds
of ants. The old scarecrow the kids had made for a school project lay abandoned, a fallen
comrade in this suburban battlefield.

## The Discovery

Then Steven saw it: the green plastic greenhouse cover, forgotten since last spring's failed
tomato experiment. As he gingerly lifted one corner, his jaw dropped. The entire underside
writhed with black bodies. White eggs clustered in neat formations. This wasn't just an
outpost—this was headquarters.

"Found the nest," Steven announced to no one, his voice carrying the gravity of a war
correspondent. The cicadas provided dramatic background music as he unleashed the Mortein,
spraying every surface with the dedication of someone debugging critical production code.

## The Aftermath

By 8am, Steven stood victorious among the carnage. The greenhouse cover lay flipped and
thoroughly doused. The scarecrow remained where it had fallen, a memorial to simpler times.
Steven's allergies were already acting up from the grass and pollen, but he'd survived.

Inside, he made his coffee—ant-free at last. He opened his laptop to document the victory
in his diary, already planning to share this tale at Monday's stand-up meeting. But as he
reached for his cup, a single scout ant crossed the kitchen bench.

"Day three," Steven whispered, watching it disappear behind the microwave. "They're back."
