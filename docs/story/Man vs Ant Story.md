# Man vs Ant

## The Coffee Crisis

Steven stood in his Brisbane kitchen at 7am on Sunday, 12 April 2026,
staring at the horror unfolding before him. A single scout ant marched
across the bench, bold as brass. Behind it, a convoy emerged from
beneath the coffee machine's drip tray.

"Impossible," Steven muttered, adjusting his faded Bunnings polo.
His wife had taken the kids to visit her parents for the school
holidays. She'd been gone three days, and already the house was
falling to invaders.

The software engineer in him kicked in. This was a P1 incident –
critical system failure, all hands on deck. Except there were no
hands. Just him, his thongs, and a can of Mortein Power Gard.

## The Battle of Easter Monday

Six days earlier, on Easter Monday, Steven had discovered the
initial breach. Ants had established a supply line from the patio
to the coffee machine. The Moccona was under siege.

"That's not a bug, that's a feature," he'd joked to himself,
before realising that yes, these were definitely bugs. Multiple
bugs. Hundreds of them.

He'd executed what he thought was a flawless deployment. Sprayed
every junction where wall met ground. Eliminated all visible
hostiles. Even lifted storage boxes to check for enemy
installations. The operation took forty-five minutes – longer
than he'd spent outside all month, given his allergies to dust,
grass, and essentially everything nature had to offer.

By sunset, the kitchen was clear. "Job done!" he'd declared to the empty house.

## The Greenhouse Revelation

But now, on this humid Sunday morning, with the temperature
already hitting 27°C, Steven faced the truth. The ants had
regrouped. They'd called reinforcements.

Armed with his Mortein and wearing zero protective equipment –
no gloves, no mask, nothing but stubbies and determination –
Steven ventured onto the concrete patio. The butcher birds,
Tina, Gina, and Zina, watched from the mango tree like
embedded war correspondents.

He discovered them everywhere. In a plastic tub – relocated to
grass, sprayed. Under the blue tarp – extracted, sprayed. Then
he saw it: the green plastic greenhouse garden bed cover.

"AAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHH!"

The cover was absolutely covered. Gingerly, Steven lifted it,
revealing what looked like a miniature city. White eggs, larvae,
workers scurrying in panic. The nest. The actual nest, laid out
flat like a suburban subdivision.

He sprayed with the precision of a man who'd spent twenty years
debugging code. Every surface, twice. The scarecrow his kids had
made last year watched from its corner, a fallen comrade in this
backyard war.

## Victory Declaration

By 8am, Steven stood victorious among the chemical clouds. The
nest was destroyed. The queen's empire had fallen. He texted his
wife: "Garden situation handled. All good here."

She replied with a single raised eyebrow emoji.

As Steven made his victory coffee, he noticed movement near the sugar bowl. A lone scout, testing the defences.

"Day 3," he whispered, reaching for his phone to Google "how to kill ants permanently."

The war wasn't over. It never was.
